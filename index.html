The Challenge

Workable, but Less Than Ideal

1

Real instructional designers work in unfamiliar domains, but students defaulted to what they already knew.

2

Students arrived with preconceived notions of what training should look like.

3

Without a real SME to interact with, students missed the most important soft-skill practice the profession demands.

What We Used to Do

The Prior Approach

"

Starting with this first project, you will be conducting a needs analysis and designing training based on a hypothetical but realistic workplace performance problem. First, come up with a hypothetical workplace and performance problem that can and should be addressed, at least in part, through training. Perhaps you are a hotel dealing with customer service problems, or maybe you are a bookstore that is not shipping out online orders on time. Choose a scenario that you have familiarity with. Choose carefully, as this is the scenario you will work with for the entire semester.

Original assignment prompt
The Idea

What if I could create chatbots that served as virtual subject matter experts?

Student Benefits
🤝
Practice SME Collaboration Authentic experience working with a subject matter expert, with no recruiting required.
🌐
Unfamiliar Territory Students engage with domains they know nothing about, mirroring real-world conditions.
⚡
Always Available On the student's schedule, removing a major logistical barrier.
